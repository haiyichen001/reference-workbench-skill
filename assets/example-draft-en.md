## Example 1 — English Journal Paper (Introduction + References)

**Source:** Sun, H.-Q., Xie, S.-S., Zeng, J.-J. & Zhuge, Y. "3D-printed concrete with in-process embedded fiber-reinforced polymer grid reinforcement." *Communications Engineering*, 5, 72 (2026). DOI: `10.1038/s44172-026-00628-1`

---

Traditional construction methods face challenges such as environmental pollution, labor shortages, and high energy consumption [CITE:ye2024size]. To address these, 3D printing technology has been increasingly applied in construction, particularly in 3D-printed concrete (3DPC), which reduces labor costs, shortens construction cycles, and saves building materials compared to traditional methods [CITE:hassan2024towards]. However, as 3DPC applications grow, the challenge of reinforcing 3DPC components has become a major concern [CITE:yu2024investigation;CITE:cao2022nail;CITE:jindal20233d;CITE:sun20253d;CITE:vanTittelboom2024micro;CITE:zhou2024experimental]. Unlike mould-cast concrete, 3DPC struggles with integrating reinforcement during the printing process due to its layer-by-layer construction. Unreinforced 3DPC components have weak flexural and tensile strength and are prone to brittle failure, limiting the potential of this technology.

To overcome this issue, researchers have proposed various reinforcement methods, including fiber reinforcement, steel reinforcement, and fiber-reinforced polymer (FRP) reinforcement [CITE:wu2022state;CITE:asprone2018rethinking]. The fiber reinforcement method, which involves adding a high content of fibers to concrete to print engineering cementitious composites (ECC), noticeably enhances the ductility and strain-hardening capacity of 3DPC components, enabling fully automated printing [CITE:han2022experimental;CITE:li2020emergence;CITE:girskas20253d;CITE:zeng2023behavior;CITE:liu2023flexural]. However, to ensure the printability of 3DPC, the fiber content cannot exceed a certain threshold, limiting the application of this method [CITE:hojati2022barbed]. Steel is widely used as reinforcement in mould-cast concrete due to its strong bonding properties with concrete [CITE:zeng2025bond;CITE:wang2022bond;CITE:ding2022experimental]. For application in 3DPC, it can be categorized into pre-installed reinforcement, in-process continuous reinforcement, and post-installed reinforcement, depending on the fabrication stage.

Regarding the pre-installed reinforcement, the most common method involves using 3DPC technology to prepare the formwork, placing the steel bars, and then pouring concrete to create an integrated structure [CITE:wu2016critical;CITE:kreiger2019development;CITE:asprone20183d]. The post-installed reinforcement method involves manually placing steel after the deposition of concrete filaments manually or using an additional machine, which is generally complicated [CITE:xiao2022bending;CITE:hass2020bending]. Although these methods successfully reinforce 3DPC structures, the process cannot be fully automated. To address this issue, a series of reinforcement methods implemented simultaneously with concrete printing have been proposed [CITE:mechtcherine20183d;CITE:ma2019micro;CITE:liu2024steel], including simultaneous printing of reinforcement bars or steel cables. The installation of steel reinforcement in 3DPC, however, is not intelligent and introduces additional porosity in concrete [CITE:ma2018printable;CITE:zeng2025bond]. In steel-reinforced concrete structures, excessive porosity can lead to corrosion of the steel by harmful ions, thus compromising the overall service life of structures [CITE:zeng2025bond]. Therefore, applying steel to 3DPC may not be an optimal solution.

More recently, fiber-reinforced polymer (FRP) reinforcement, which possesses excellent tensile strength and durability, has been adopted as reinforcement for 3DPC structures [CITE:zeng2023behavior;CITE:zeng2025bond;CITE:zhang2024flexural;CITE:zeng20233d;CITE:sun2022shear;CITE:zhou2023bond;CITE:lu2023durability;CITE:zhang2021feasibility;CITE:zeng2022flexural]. The author team proposed enhancing the flexural performance of 3D printed high-performance concrete plates by placing FRP grids and FRP bars between the printed layers [CITE:zeng2023behavior;CITE:zeng20233d]. Their results demonstrated that FRP noticeably improves both the flexural strength and ductility of 3DPC plates. Similarly, a previous study explored the bond performance between BFRP bars and 3DPC [CITE:sun2021bond]. It was found that the bond strength of the 3D printed specimens was lower than that of the mould-cast specimens, and the damage pattern of the BFRP bar reinforced printed concrete was similar to that of reinforced mould-cast concrete [CITE:zeng20233d;CITE:sun2021bond]. Although these studies have highlighted the potential of FRP for reinforcing 3DPC, a key challenge remains: fully automated printing has not yet been achieved, as FRP still needs to be placed manually.

On the other hand, using functional materials for functionally graded concrete (FGC) is an innovative approach to optimally use construction materials, which is achieved by introducing different types of materials or additives with a gradient in performance [CITE:gao2023thermal]. This design allows FGC to differentiate performance in different parts of a structure to meet specific performance needs. Despite the noticeable advantages of FGC, the existing production process is still immature, which limits its practical application to a certain extent. 3D printing technology can realize the precise preparation of FGC components owing to the layer-by-layer construction technology, which overcomes the limitations of traditional manufacturing methods [CITE:liu2023flexural;CITE:gao2023thermal].

## Example 2 — Chinese Doctoral Thesis (English Abstract + Key References)

**Source:** Lin, J. "AI-Driven Intelligent Synthesis of Ce-Based MOFs and Optimization of Their Catalytic Hydrogenation Performance." Doctoral Dissertation, University of Science and Technology Beijing, 2025. (Reconstructed from CNKI PDF; model names and reference details are approximate.)

---

Metal-organic frameworks (MOFs), with their atomically designable structures, ultra-high specific surface areas, and diverse chemical functionalities, have been widely investigated for catalysis, adsorption, separation, and energy conversion [CITE:zhou2023mofReview]. However, their synthesis and optimization still rely primarily on empirically driven trial-and-error experimentation. Synthesis condition optimization depends on iterative experiments with high process complexity, substantial resource consumption, and low efficiency, constraining the efficient development and practical application of MOFs [CITE:wang2024mofChallenge]. Current research also faces difficulties in multi-objective optimization, ineffective use of experimental data, and challenging elucidation of structure-performance relationships [CITE:li2023multiObj].

It is therefore necessary to develop artificial intelligence (AI)-based intelligent R&D strategies to shift MOF material development from empirical exploration to data-driven and knowledge-guided approaches [CITE:butler2024mlMaterials]. This dissertation focuses on rare-earth metal-organic frameworks (REMOFs) and their supported metal composite catalysts for dicyclopentadiene (DCPD) hydrogenation, comprehensively employing machine learning (ML), large language models (LLMs), and related AI technologies to conduct systematic studies around synthesis parameter optimization, experimental scheme design, and mechanistic analysis [CITE:jablonka2023mlMof;CITE:bran2025llmChemistry].

A machine learning-assisted closed-loop multi-objective optimization workflow was established for UiO-66(Ce), enabling the identification of optimal synthesis conditions achieving defect concentration >40% and thermal stability >300 deg C within only 2 optimization rounds [CITE:feng2024defectEng]. An LLM-based agent MOFs_n was developed with modules for data interpretation, mechanistic reasoning, and experimental planning, integrating retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) linked to a materials knowledge base [CITE:boiko2023llmAgent]. A multi-LLM collaborative reasoning framework combining graph retrieval-augmented generation (GRAG) was proposed, using a round-table conference mechanism with models including GPT-4.1, DeepSeek-V3.2, GLM-4.6, and Qwen3-MAX [CITE:kim2025grag].

The optimized Ni@Ba/UiO-66(Ce) catalyst achieved 100% DCPD conversion and 100% THDCPD selectivity at 70 deg C, 2 MPa H2, significantly outperforming most reported high-loading Ni/MOF catalysts. Characterization indicates that increased defect concentration, Ba doping, and electron-rich Ni active sites synergistically enhance the adsorption and activation of H2 and C=C bonds [CITE:chen2025niSite]. This framework links knowledge graph retrieval, multi-LLM reasoning, and experimental validation into an closed-loop pipeline for materials composition design and synthesis planning.
